# Culture Codes

A fast lookup for how color, symbol, number and layout choices land in different cultural contexts.
Always cross-check here before finalizing a visual direction that will run in more than one market.

## Color Meaning (by region)

| Color | China (mainland) | Japan | Western (US/EU) | India | Middle East | South Africa |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Red | festive, luck, prosperity | vitality, passion | danger, passion, urgency | purity (weddings), prosperity | caution, sometimes evil | mourning |
| White | funerals, mourning | purity, sacred | clean, minimal, modern | peace, but also mourning | purity | (see red) |
| Black | formal, serious, premium | formal, youth-subculture | luxury, rebellion | evil, darkness (often avoided) | formal, mourning | mourning |
| Yellow | royalty (historically), warmth | courage, sometimes treachery | caution, happy, cheap (US) | holy, sacred | mourning (in some) | (varies) |
| Green | youth, growth (politically loaded in some contexts) | nature, eternal | freshness, eco, money | nature, Islam-adjacent | Islam, holy | (varies) |
| Blue | trust, technology | trust, calm | trust, professional, corporate | Krishna, divine | protection from evil | trust |
| Purple | nobility (ancient), today = creative | nobility, mystery | royalty, creativity, spirituality | nobility, spirituality | (varies) | mourning (specific groups) |
| Pink | feminine, playful, youthful | cherry blossom, femininity | feminine, romantic | reserved use | (varies) | (varies) |
| Gold | wealth, imperial | wealth, tradition | wealth, awards | wealth, prosperity | wealth, sacred | (varies) |

Rule: whenever you pick red/white/black/green, stop for 3 seconds and think about the target market before committing.

## Symbols & Numbers

### Symbols

| Symbol | China | Japan | Western | India |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Owl | bad omen | wisdom | wisdom | associated with death |
| Dragon | imperial luck, power | protective, wise | threat to be slain | deity-adjacent |
| Crow | inauspicious in many contexts | sacred messenger (yatagarasu) | death/bad luck | ancestors |
| Lotus | purity, cultural classic | purity, Buddhism | exotic | divine, core symbol |
| Skull | death, avoid | death, fashion in subcultures | mortality, rebel fashion | Shiva, transformative |

### Numbers

| Number | China / HK / TW | Japan / Korea | Western | Middle East |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | avoid — 死 (death) | avoid — same reason | no taboo | no taboo |
| 7 | lucky | lucky | lucky | lucky |
| 8 | lucky — 发 (fortune) | neutral | neutral | neutral |
| 13 | neutral | neutral | often unlucky | neutral |
| 666 | auspicious (smoothness) | neutral | often taboo (religious) | neutral |
| 9 | imperial, lasting | avoid — sounds like 苦 (suffering) in Japanese | neutral | neutral |

Small number swaps can change conversion sharply. Use local conventions for pricing endings (8 in China, .99 in Western, etc.) instead of copying a global template.

## Script & Layout Direction

| Script system | Reading direction | Layout implication |
|---|---|---|
| Latin | left-to-right | Z-pattern and F-pattern scans apply |
| Chinese (simplified) | left-to-right (modern), top-to-bottom (traditional vertical) | For heritage brands vertical is a stylistic option, not a default |
| Japanese | left-to-right (modern), right-to-left / top-to-bottom (traditional) | Manga / novel contexts often RTL top-bottom |
| Arabic | right-to-left | The entire UI mirrors. Navigation, arrows, progress bars all flip |
| Hebrew | right-to-left | Same mirror rule as Arabic |

Do not only flip text. Flip icons, arrows, progress indicators, and usually photos of faces.

## East-Asian Aesthetic Conventions

- **Negative space is value**, not emptiness. Do not fill it just because Western templates say to.
- **Asymmetric balance** is preferred over perfect symmetry.
- **Material restraint** — showing the texture of paper, wood, or ceramic usually outperforms glossy rendering.
- **Typography hierarchy** — Song/Mincho for formal heritage; Hei/Gothic for modern; handwriting for warmth. Do not mix more than two families.
- **Avoid costume-drama clichés** for "new Chinese" or "Japanese aesthetic" — dragons, cherry blossoms, geisha, and temple gates are safe but lazy. Prefer material and light cues.

## Western Aesthetic Conventions

- **Grid obedience** is read as "professional". Breaking it is read as "intentional".
- **Strong single focal point** is preferred over distributed attention.
- **Sans-serif = modern; serif = authoritative or editorial**. Mixing one from each family is standard.
- **Realistic photography** with imperfection beats idealized illustration for "trust".

## Middle-Eastern Aesthetic Conventions

- **Dense geometric pattern** is celebrated, not cluttered.
- **Calligraphy as art**, not just text — Arabic script often carries visual weight that rivals imagery.
- **Avoid pork, alcohol, and immodest exposure** in imagery for Muslim-majority markets.
- RTL layout is the default assumption.

## Generational Visual Codes

| Generation | Visual codes that land | Anti-patterns (will trigger rejection) |
|---|---|---|
| Gen Z (1997–2012) | Y2K revival, hyper-saturation, glitch, meme-literacy, hand-made imperfection, authentic low-fi | over-polished corporate stock, paternalistic copy, "trying to be relatable", preachy messaging |
| Millennial (1981–1996) | minimalism, gradients, soft sans-serif, lifestyle photography, wellness cues | maximalist chaos, heavy ornament, corporate stiffness |
| Gen X (1965–1980) | clear hierarchy, trustworthy photography, practical tone, functional over decorative | Gen-Z meme style, ironic detachment, over-abstract |
| Boomer (1946–1964) | larger type, high contrast, familiar visual metaphors, concrete imagery | avant-garde typography, tiny type, obscure references |

Do not treat these as absolute. They are **default priors** that you adjust once you know the specific audience within a generation.

## Sub-culture Notes (use sparingly and only when relevant)

- **Xiaohongshu (小红书)**: vertical 3:4 or 1:1, tactile realism, calm warm palette, product-in-scene over product-on-white, Chinese copy with plenty of negative space. Hard-sell visual kills the post.
- **Douyin / TikTok**: 9:16, strong first-frame hook, high-contrast text, faces > objects.
- **LinkedIn Western**: desaturated photography of real humans at work, cool palette, restrained copy on image.
- **Instagram lifestyle**: warm palette, golden hour, shallow DOF, personality over product.

## Red-Flag Checklist (run before delivery)

1. Any color/symbol flagged in this document for the target market?
2. Any number with negative connotation in pricing, model names, or room numbers?
3. Script direction and layout mirror correct?
4. Imagery respects local modesty and religious norms?
5. Reference style not borrowed from the "wrong" culture (e.g. sakura for a Chinese brand)?
